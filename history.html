125+ Years in Construction

Building Chicago’s Story Since 1892

Miner & East is one of Chicago’s oldest general contracting firms, with a continuous history that stretches back more than a century.

In over 125 years of general contracting and construction management, Miner & East has learned what defines true service: flexibility, timeliness, technical skill, and competitive pricing. These are the fundamentals clients expect—and the fundamentals the firm consistently delivers.

Just as important are the energy, enthusiasm, and understanding of each client’s needs and goals that Miner & East brings to every project, from emergency reconstruction to complex new construction.

What we stand for

  • Over 125 years of continuous operation
  • Full-service general contracting & construction management
  • Deep experience in insurance loss, reconstruction, and new construction
1892–Early 1900s

From Carpenter & Builder to Miner & East

Founding & early leadership

The roots of Miner & East date back to John McInnis – Carpenter & Builder, founded in 1892. Operating in the early 1900s, the firm was led by founder John McInnis alongside two superintendents: John E. Miner and Arvid East.

When John McInnis retired, Miner and East acquired the business and formed Miner & East, marking the beginning of a partnership that would carry the company through the next stage of its growth.

Ownership begins

  • 1892 – John McInnis launches the original firm
  • Early 1900s – Miner & East emerge as core leaders
  • Transition – McInnis retires, Miner & East assume ownership
1920s–1930s

Miner & East Incorporated

A growing partnership

After the original transition from John McInnis, the company continued to evolve. Following his graduation from the University of Chicago, John J. McInnis, son of the founder, joined the firm.

In the early 1920s, Arvid East purchased John E. Miner’s shares and, in 1926, John J. McInnis became a partner with East.

In 1932, Miner & East formally incorporated. For many years, the firm was operated by Arvid East and John J. McInnis, providing steady leadership and a reputation for dependable construction services.

Key milestones

  • Early 1920s – Arvid East acquires Miner’s shares
  • 1926 – John J. McInnis becomes a partner
  • 1932 – Miner & East incorporates
1940s–1950s

A New Generation After World War II

Dowell & McInnis leadership

Following service as a Marine in the Pacific Theater during World War II, Sanford (Sandy) J. Dowell joined Miner & East in 1949.

After graduating from the University of Pennsylvania and fulfilling his Marine military service, John (Jack) W. McInnis, grandson of founder John J. McInnis, joined the company in 1955.

Both Sandy Dowell and Jack McInnis became partners with Arvid East and John J. McInnis, positioning the firm for significant growth in the decades ahead.

Leadership expands

  • 1949 – Sandy Dowell joins after Marine service
  • 1955 – Jack McInnis joins after military service & university
  • Dowell & McInnis become partners with East and McInnis
1960s–1980s

From Founders to Long-Term Partners

Retirements & next generation

Arvid East retired in 1960, followed by John J. McInnis. Sandy Dowell and Jack McInnis continued to operate the business as partners through the 1960s and 1970s.

In the late 1960s, Michael S. Dowell, son of Sandy Dowell, began his career with Miner & East after graduating from Monmouth College.

In the early 1970s, Daniel J. Dowell, Sandy’s second son, joined the firm after graduating from the University of Arizona, further extending the Dowell family’s role in the company.

Sandy Dowell retired in 1983 as president after 34 years with the firm.

Continuity in leadership

  • 1960 – Retirement of Arvid East
  • 1960s–1970s – Dowell & McInnis lead the firm
  • Late 1960s–1970s – Michael & Daniel Dowell join Miner & East
  • 1983 – Sandy Dowell retires as president
1980s–2000s

Carrying the Legacy Forward

Transitions & new directions

Jack McInnis retired in 2005 as chairman of the firm after an extraordinary 50-year career with Miner & East.

Michael S. Dowell devoted 38 years to the company before passing away in 2005 after a heroic five-year battle with cancer.

In 2002, Christopher S. Dowell (son of Daniel J. Dowell and grandson of Sandy Dowell) joined the firm after graduating from the University of Kansas.

Eric D. Koertge came to Miner & East in 2005 after graduating from the University of Illinois and spending seven years with W.E. O’Neil Construction Company, a major Chicago-area general contractor.

In 2012, Miner & East separated the consulting arm of the firm and formed Miner & East LLC, reflecting the company’s deep expertise in specialized construction consulting.

New chapter

  • 2002 – Christopher S. Dowell joins the firm
  • 2005 – Eric D. Koertge joins after major GC experience
  • 2012 – Consulting arm becomes Miner & East LLC
Today

Miner & East Leadership Today

Carrying a 125-year legacy forward

Today, Daniel J. Dowell serves as owner and president of Miner & East, Inc. General Contractors.

Christopher S. Dowell is Executive Vice President, and Eric D. Koertge serves as Vice President.

Together, Daniel J. Dowell, Christopher S. Dowell, and Eric Koertge are principal owners of Miner & East LLC, Construction Consultants, blending deep heritage with modern technical expertise.

Across general contracting, reconstruction, and consulting, Miner & East remains committed to delivering the same values that have defined the firm for generations: honesty, craftsmanship, and a client-first approach.

Current principals

  • Daniel J. Dowell – Owner & President
  • Christopher S. Dowell – Executive Vice President
  • Eric D. Koertge – Vice President
  • All three – Principal owners of Miner & East LLC
